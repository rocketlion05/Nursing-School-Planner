California has one of the largest and most competitive sets of BSN (Bachelor of Science in Nursing) programs in the country, spanning the UC system, more than a dozen Cal State campuses, and strong private universities. The catch is that "getting in" means very different things depending on the school: some admit you straight out of high school with no nursing-specific cutoff, while others run impacted, points-based competitions with hard GPA and exam floors. Here's how to navigate it.

## 1. Know which type of program you're applying to

The single most important step in California is figuring out the entry model, because it changes everything else.

- **Direct-admit / freshman-entry programs** admit you into nursing as an incoming first-year student, with no separate nursing application and no entrance exam. **UCLA** (Joe C. Wen School of Nursing) and **UC Irvine** work this way for freshmen, as do **CSU Fullerton**, the **University of San Francisco**, and **Point Loma Nazarene University**. These have no nursing-specific GPA cutoff published for freshman entry (UC Irvine lists a 3.0 cumulative GPA for freshman applicants), but they are extraordinarily competitive. UCLA, for example, reports roughly 40 freshman seats against 6,000-plus applications.
- **Impacted upper-division / transfer programs** admit you after you complete prerequisites, usually through a competitive point ranking. Most Cal States and the public flagships fall here, and this is where the GPA and TEAS numbers below apply.

If a school's nursing page says "no minimum GPA" or "no entrance exam," that's a real, useful fact, not missing data. It usually means admission rides on overall selectivity or impaction criteria instead.

## 2. Hit the GPA targets

Among California's impacted programs, prerequisite and science GPA minimums cluster around **3.0**, but admitted students typically score far higher. As of 2026, confirm each cutoff on the school's official page, since impacted programs adjust criteria often.

- **CSU San Bernardino** requires a **3.5 overall cumulative GPA** plus a 3.0 prerequisite GPA.
- **CSU Long Beach** and **CSU Los Angeles** both require a **3.25** prerequisite GPA, with a B or better (3.0) in science courses.
- **San Diego State** requires a **3.2 cumulative GPA**, and **Biola University** a 3.2 cumulative.
- Many others, including **Fresno State, CSU Bakersfield, CSU Chico, CSU Stanislaus, Azusa Pacific, Loma Linda, and Mount Saint Mary's**, set a **3.0** floor. Several schools also compute a separate nursing or prerequisite GPA that runs even higher in practice (Sacramento State and San Jose State, for instance, calculate prerequisite GPAs of 3.3+).

A grade of **C or better in every prerequisite** is essentially universal, and some programs (CSU LA) enforce a strict no-retake rule.

## 3. Prepare for the entrance exam (usually the TEAS)

Most impacted California programs use the **ATI TEAS**, but the required score varies widely, so target the specific school:

- **San Jose State** sets the highest published bar in the data at **84%**.
- **San Diego State** and **CSU Chico** require **80%**.
- **San Francisco State** requires **78%**, **Sacramento State, Fresno State** require **75%**, and **CSU Bakersfield** **72%**.
- **CSU San Marcos (71%), CSU Los Angeles, CSU San Bernardino, CSU Stanislaus, Biola (70%)**, and **California Baptist (64%)** round out the lower tier.

A few schools use no academic entrance exam at all. **CSULB** and **CSU Chico** instead use (or also use) the **Casper** situational-judgment test. And as noted above, UCLA, UC Irvine, CSU Fullerton, USF, Point Loma, and Azusa Pacific publish no entrance-exam requirement. ## 4. Complete the prerequisite sequence

California prerequisites are fairly standardized: **Anatomy & Physiology I and II, Microbiology, Chemistry, Statistics, and English Composition**, with several schools (CSU LA, Sacramento State, San Jose State, Loma Linda) also requiring **Nutrition** and a **lifespan / human development** course. Important details to watch:

- Many programs cap how old science coursework can be, often **5 to 10 years** (CSU Bakersfield uses 10 years; USF and Mount Saint Mary's require recent science grades).
- Some private and direct-entry schools (USF, Point Loma) ask for a higher grade, like **B or better**, in the core sciences.
- A handful require fewer prerequisites because coursework is built into the degree.

## 5. Plan your application timeline

California cycles are scattered and often run through **NursingCAS** in addition to Cal State Apply or the UC Application. A few examples from the data:

- **UCLA**: UC Application due Nov 30, nursing supplemental due Jan 15 (Fall only).
- **San Diego State** and **CSU Fullerton**: windows opening Aug 1 with Nov 30 deadlines.
- **CSU LA**: NursingCAS opens Apr 1, deadline May 31 (Fall only).
- Several Cal States (Sacramento State, Fresno State, CSU Stanislaus) admit each Fall and Spring on separate short windows.

Map every deadline now. Many impacted programs admit only once a year, and missing a single window can cost you a full cycle.

## 6. Round out a competitive file

Meeting minimums rarely guarantees a seat in California's impacted programs, which rank applicants on points.

- Several schools award **bonus points** for things like second-language ability, healthcare experience, or in-service-area residency (CSU Stanislaus is explicit about this).
- A focused **personal statement** matters, and a few programs (San Jose State, CSU Bakersfield) interview or require essays.
- Keep your **background check, drug screen, and immunizations** ready, since impacted programs (CSU Chico, CSU San Bernardino) often require them at or shortly after admission.
