A 3.0 cumulative GPA is one of the most common floors in nursing school admissions. Plenty of accredited BSN (Bachelor of Science in Nursing) programs publish a minimum cumulative GPA of 3.0, and many set it even lower. If you are sitting right around a 3.0, the good news is that you are eligible to apply to a wide range of real programs.

The important caveat: a published minimum is not a guarantee of admission. Most BSN programs are competitive, and admitted students often land well above the stated floor. A 3.0 gets your application read; it does not get you a seat by itself. Treat the minimum as the door being open, then build the strongest possible application to walk through it.

Every school below has a **published GPA floor at or below 3.0** that we have confirmed from official sources. Cutoffs change often, so always confirm current requirements on the official admissions page or the live program page before you apply.

## How to read this list

For each school we list the **published minimum cumulative GPA** and the **entrance exam** it requires (TEAS, HESI A2, or none). A few things to keep in mind:

- Many programs also require a separate **prerequisite or science GPA**, which can be higher than the cumulative floor.
- Some schools publish a low GPA minimum but are still highly competitive because they rank applicants by GPA and exam score.
- Exam requirements vary widely. Some programs require no entrance exam at all; others want a specific TEAS or HESI percentage.

## A 3.0 minimum does not mean easy admission

Several schools on this list publish a low floor but admit competitively. Programs like Baylor, Emory, UNC Chapel Hill, and St. Mary's set a modest GPA minimum yet receive far more qualified applicants than they have seats. In those cases the published number tells you who may apply, not who gets in. If you are at a 3.0, prioritize:

1. **Prerequisite and science GPA.** Many programs weigh these separately, and a strong science GPA can offset a borderline cumulative number.
2. **Your entrance exam.** A high TEAS or HESI A2 score is one of the fastest ways to strengthen a 3.0 application.
3. **Healthcare experience and a focused essay**, which matter most in holistic review.
