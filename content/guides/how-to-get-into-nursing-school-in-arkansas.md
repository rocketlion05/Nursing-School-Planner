Arkansas has a focused set of BSN (Bachelor of Science in Nursing) programs, anchored by UAMS, the University of Arkansas, and the University of Central Arkansas, plus strong regional and private options. Because the field is smaller, knowing each school's specific requirements gives you a real advantage. Here's how to get in.

## 1. Hit the GPA targets

Arkansas BSN programs look at both your cumulative GPA and your prerequisite GPA. Typical minimums range from **2.5 to 3.0**, but admitted students usually land well above the floor.

- **UAMS** requires a 2.5 cumulative and prerequisite GPA (and waives the TEAS when both GPAs are 3.4+).
- The **University of Arkansas (Eleanor Mann School of Nursing)** expects roughly a 3.0, with competitive admits scoring around 70+ on the TEAS.
- A grade of **C or better** in each prerequisite is standard.

## 2. Prepare for the TEAS (and check the exceptions)

Most Arkansas programs use the **ATI TEAS** entrance exam. A few important nuances:

- The **University of Central Arkansas requires no entrance exam at all**: admission is GPA-driven.
- The **University of Arkansas requires both the TEAS and CASPer** (a situational-judgment test).
- Several programs accept a "Proficient" TEAS level (around 59%+) rather than a fixed numeric cutoff.

Always confirm the exact requirement per school, because they differ more than you'd expect across Arkansas.

## 3. Complete the prerequisite sequence

Common Arkansas prerequisites include Anatomy & Physiology I & II, Microbiology, Chemistry, Statistics, Nutrition, Human Growth & Development, and English Composition. Note that the University of Arkansas uses College Algebra rather than Statistics. Science courses older than a set number of years (often 5–7) may need to be retaken.

## 4. Plan your application timeline

Arkansas programs run on varied cycles: some admit once per year, others each Fall and Spring. The University of Arkansas applies through **NursingCAS**. Map out each school's deadline early; missing a single annual cycle can cost you a full year.

## 5. Round out a competitive file

- A focused personal statement helps in holistic review.
- Healthcare experience (CNA, volunteering) strengthens borderline applications.
- Keep immunizations, background check, and transcripts ready, since many programs require them at or shortly after admission.
