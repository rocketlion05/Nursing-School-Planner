Louisiana has a deep bench of BSN (Bachelor of Science in Nursing) programs, from the academic health science center at LSU Health New Orleans to large regional publics like Southeastern Louisiana University and McNeese, plus a strong set of private and faith-based schools. Requirements vary more than you might expect from one campus to the next, so knowing each school's specific bar gives you a real edge. Here's how to get in. Want a quick read on your fit? ## 1. Hit the GPA targets

Most Louisiana BSN programs gate admission on a cumulative or prerequisite GPA, and the floors cluster between **2.5 and 3.0**. Admitted students typically land well above the minimum because seats are competitive.

- **LSU Health New Orleans** and **Southeastern Louisiana University** both require a **3.0** prerequisite or cumulative GPA, and **Northwestern State University** also sets a 3.0 minimum.
- **Nicholls State University** and **Franciscan Missionaries of Our Lady University (FranU)** require **2.75**.
- **Southern University and A&M College**, **Grambling State University**, and the **University of Louisiana at Monroe (Kitty DeGree School of Nursing)** require **2.8**, with Grambling also asking for a 2.8 science GPA.
- **University of Louisiana at Lafayette** sets the bar at **2.6**, **Louisiana Christian University** at **2.6**, and **Southern University at New Orleans** at **2.7**.
- **Loyola University New Orleans**, **University of Holy Cross**, and **Herzing University** require **2.5**.

A grade of **C or better** in each prerequisite is the norm, and some schools want higher: LSU Health New Orleans expects a **B- or better** in each science course. These figures hold as of 2026; confirm the current number on each school's official page.

## 2. Know which entrance exam your schools use

Louisiana is split between the **ATI TEAS** and the **HESI A2**, and some programs use neither.

- **HESI A2** schools include **LSU Health New Orleans**, which requires a minimum **80%** cumulative score plus an essay and online interview, and **Chamberlain University**, which accepts the HESI A2 (or ACT/SAT/TEAS) with no published cutoff.
- **TEAS** schools include **McNeese State University** (minimum **68%**), **Grambling State** (minimum **64%**), **Louisiana Christian University** (minimum **65%**), and **Herzing University** (composite **58**).
- Several TEAS programs ask for a **"Proficient" level** rather than a fixed number, including **University of Holy Cross**, **Nicholls State**, **Southern University and A&M**, and **Southeastern University at New Orleans (SUNO)**. **FranU** accepts a TEAS "Proficient" or a HESI A2 "Satisfactory" level.
- Some strong programs require **no entrance exam at all**, including **Southeastern Louisiana University**, the **University of Louisiana at Monroe**, and **Loyola University New Orleans**.

Because the same exam can carry very different cutoffs across campuses, confirm the exact requirement per school. If you're deciding which test to prep for, see [TEAS vs HESI A2](/guides/teas-vs-hesi-a2-which-nursing-entrance-exam).

## 3. Complete the prerequisite sequence

Common Louisiana prerequisites include **Anatomy & Physiology I & II, Microbiology, Chemistry, Statistics, Nutrition, Developmental (Lifespan) Psychology, and English Composition**. Watch for school-specific twists:

- **Northwestern State** uses **College Algebra or Finite Math** rather than Statistics.
- **LSU Health New Orleans** wants a **B- or better** in each science (Biology with lab, Chemistry, Microbiology).
- A few programs build prerequisites into the curriculum: **Chamberlain University** advertises a 122-credit track with no separate prerequisite courses.

Science courses older than a set number of years may need to be retaken; **Southern University and A&M**, for example, requires science and nursing courses to be under 5 years old. ## 4. Plan your application timeline

Louisiana programs run on varied cycles, and several admit more than once a year.

- **Southeastern Louisiana** uses **September 1 for Spring entry and February 1 for Fall** (Traditional track).
- **University of Louisiana at Monroe** sets the **first Monday of February** for Fall and the **second Monday of September** for Spring.
- **Southern University and A&M** uses **February 15 (Fall) and September 15 (Spring)**, applying through **NursingCAS**.
- **Grambling State** listed an **April 13, 2026** deadline for its Fall 2026 cohort, and **FranU** uses **January 30** (August class) and **August 30** (January class).
- **LSU Health New Orleans** uses an **August 31** deadline for Spring entry via NursingCAS and admits Fall and Spring.
- A few schools, including **Herzing** and **Chamberlain**, use **rolling admissions**.

Map out each school's deadline early; missing a single annual cycle can cost you a full year. Note that some Louisiana schools, including **McNeese** and **Dillard University**, do not publish all their dates and cutoffs openly, so always verify on the official page.

## 5. Round out a competitive file

Meeting the minimums rarely guarantees a seat at the more selective Louisiana programs, so strengthen the rest of your file.

- A focused **personal statement or essay** matters where schools use holistic review; LSU Health New Orleans and several others require an essay or interview.
- **Healthcare experience** (CNA, volunteering, scribing) helps borderline applications stand out.
- Keep **immunizations, a background check, and transcripts** ready, since many programs require clearance through the Louisiana State Board of Nursing before clinicals begin.
- Understand **direct-admit vs. upper-division** structures: schools like **LSU Health New Orleans** admit students to the upper division after prerequisites, while others such as **Loyola New Orleans** and **Herzing** offer direct-admit paths. Knowing which model a school uses tells you when your application really counts.
