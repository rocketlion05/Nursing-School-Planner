North Carolina offers a deep field of BSN (Bachelor of Science in Nursing) programs, from the flagship at UNC Chapel Hill to large public schools like East Carolina University, UNC Charlotte, and UNC Greensboro, plus private options such as Queens University of Charlotte and Gardner-Webb. ## 1. Hit the GPA targets

North Carolina BSN programs weigh both your cumulative GPA and your prerequisite (math/science) GPA. Published minimums in the data range from the high 2s to the low 3s, but admitted students usually land well above the floor.

- **UNC Chapel Hill** requires a 2.8 cumulative and 2.8 science GPA (its accelerated ABSN requires 3.0), with sciences earned at B- or better within five years.
- **East Carolina University** lists a 2.75 minimum cumulative GPA, but it is highly competitive: admitted students average roughly 3.4+.
- **UNC Charlotte** and **Western Carolina University** both require a 3.0, and UNC Charlotte expects a B or higher in science prerequisites.
- **North Carolina Central University** sets a higher bar at 3.2 overall and 3.2 math/science.
- **Winston-Salem State University** uses a holistic review with a 2.6 cumulative and 2.6 science minimum.

These are floors, not targets. As of 2026, confirm each cutoff on the school's official page, since competitive admits often score far above the minimum.

## 2. Know whether your school wants an entrance exam

Entrance-exam requirements split sharply across North Carolina, so check before you study.

- **No exam required:** UNC Chapel Hill, UNC Charlotte, and Appalachian State University admit on GPA, prerequisites, and essays rather than a standardized nursing exam.
- **TEAS schools:** UNC Greensboro, UNC Wilmington, Western Carolina, Winston-Salem State, NC A&T, and several others use the **ATI TEAS**. Many publish no fixed numeric cutoff. Where a minimum exists in the data, it's listed: **NC Central** requires a TEAS of at least 70, while private **Queens University of Charlotte** and **Gardner-Webb** each accept a TEAS of 65+ (Queens and Gardner-Webb also accept SAT/ACT alternatives).
- **HESI A2 school:** **East Carolina University** uses the HESI A2 (max two attempts) with no published minimum score.

If your target school uses the TEAS or HESI, [TEAS vs HESI A2](/guides/teas-vs-hesi-a2-which-nursing-entrance-exam) breaks down the differences. Always confirm the current exam and any minimum on the official page, since these vary more here than in most states.

## 3. Complete the prerequisite sequence

Common North Carolina prerequisites in the data include Anatomy & Physiology I & II, Microbiology, Chemistry, Statistics, Nutrition, Human Growth & Development (lifespan), and English Composition, though the exact list differs by school.

- **UNC Chapel Hill** centers on A&P I/II, Microbiology, Chemistry, and Statistics.
- **UNC Charlotte** and **UNC Greensboro** require a fuller set including Nutrition, lifespan development, and English Composition.
- **ECU** notes that its math requirement (MATH 1065) is college algebra rather than statistics.
- **Queens University of Charlotte** does not require Chemistry but does require Developmental Psychology.

Many schools require a grade of **C or better** in each prerequisite, and several (including NC Central and NC A&T) note that science courses expire after about five years. Our [nursing prerequisites checklist](/guides/nursing-school-prerequisites-checklist) helps you map the sequence early.

## 4. Understand direct-admit vs upper-division pathways

North Carolina programs admit students at different points, and this shapes your timeline.

- Several schools, including **UNC Charlotte**, **UNC Greensboro**, and **Appalachian State**, run **upper-division** pre-licensure BSNs: you complete prerequisites first and apply to enter the nursing major later (App State admits at the end of sophomore year). UNCG and Queens also offer **direct-admit** pathways for strong incoming students, and UNC Chapel Hill has an "Assured Admissions" route for exceptional NC residents.
- Watch for extra steps. UNC Charlotte and Appalachian State require **CNA I certification**, and a few schools (Western Carolina, Fayetteville State) factor in interviews, essays, or additional tests.

## 5. Plan your application timeline

Deadlines in the data span the calendar, and missing an annual cycle can cost you a full year.

- **UNC Chapel Hill:** mid-December for the traditional BSN (applications open in August).
- **East Carolina University:** February 1 for Fall, September 1 for Spring.
- **UNC Charlotte:** Fall window Nov 15 to Jan 31; Spring window Jun 15 to Aug 31.
- **UNC Greensboro:** October 1 (application period August to October).
- **UNC Wilmington:** December 15 for Fall, July 1 for Spring.
- **Queens University of Charlotte:** priority April 1, final July 31 for Fall 2026.

Several schools apply through NursingCAS. Map every deadline now, and confirm each on the official page as of 2026.

## 6. Round out a competitive file

- A focused personal statement and strong essays matter for the many North Carolina schools using holistic or scored review.
- Healthcare experience (CNA, volunteering) strengthens borderline applications, and CNA I is outright required at some schools.
- Keep immunizations, background checks, and transcripts ready, since programs often require them at or shortly after admission.
