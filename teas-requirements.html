TEAS Score Requirements for Nursing School

TEAS score requirements can affect whether your nursing school application is reviewed, ranked, or waitlisted. Use this guide to understand minimum scores, competitive scores, and how to plan your retake strategy.

How nursing schools use TEAS scores

The ATI TEAS is an entrance exam used by many nursing programs to measure academic readiness in reading, math, science, and English language usage. Some schools use a hard minimum TEAS score requirement, while others use TEAS results as one part of a point system with GPA, prerequisites, experience, and essays.

A posted minimum score only tells you the lowest score a program may accept. A competitive TEAS score is often higher, especially for selective BSN programs or programs with limited seats.

Quick rule of thumb

If a school lists a TEAS minimum, aim at least several points above it. A score that barely meets the minimum may keep you eligible, but it may not make your application competitive.

What is a good TEAS score for nursing school?

A good TEAS score depends on the program. Community college ADN programs may have different score ranges than competitive university BSN programs. Many pre-nursing students use these ranges when building a school list:

  • Below 65: May limit options unless the program has a lower minimum or uses a broader review.
  • 65 to 74: Often eligible for some programs, but may be less competitive at selective schools.
  • 75 to 84: A stronger range for many nursing school applications.
  • 85 and above: Often a strong score, especially when paired with a solid science GPA.

How to improve your TEAS score requirements match

  1. Compare the TEAS score requirements for every program on your list.
  2. Separate minimum scores from competitive scores whenever the school provides both.
  3. Review your weakest TEAS sections first, especially science and math.
  4. Schedule practice tests under timed conditions before paying for a retake.
  5. Check retake rules, waiting periods, attempt limits, and whether the school accepts superscores.
